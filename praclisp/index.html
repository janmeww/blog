Programming Tutorials

Welcome back!

You have probably arrived here after grokking my ten-episode free Lua programming tutorials on Quora.

If so, while I am writing the rest of the Lua tutorials, I have something for you in the meantime.(If not, then I suggest you head to my Lua tutorial space on Quora and grok it before coming back here.)

As a future programmer, it is imperative that you know at least one Lisp dialect so as to take that knowledge and delve deeper into the wonderful world of functional programming.

To this end, I highly recommend Scheme. You can go all the way from chapter one to twelve in this freely-available and well-written Scheme tutorial after which you will need a drillbook to exercise your newly-acquired knowledge before proceeding further.

Since there were no ueful drillbooks available anywhere, I wrote a Scheme practice drills which goes from zero all the way to tail recursion, corresponding nicely with chapter 12 of Shido’s tutorial.

My goal was to create practice problems that are slightly more difficult, varied, and in-depth to allow you to master it, not just know it. I hope I have succeeded in that goal. :)
